What is a REIT?


A Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) is a company that owns or finances real estate that produces income. REITs allow investors to earn dividends without having to buy, manage, or finance properties.


EQR in particular can be affected by less affordable housing, as more people rent due to high prices or mortgage rates.

Mortage REITs, such as NLY, are sensitive to rises in mortgage rates (can be observed in Federal Funds Rate).

INVH is another residential REIT, but specializes in homes; EQR specializes in urban/suburban apartments.


This app focuses on:


  • NLY - Annaly Capital Management
  • EQR - Equity Residential
  • INVH - Invitation Homes

Economic Indicators Used:


  • Federal Funds Rate - Affects borrowing and mortgage rates
  • CPI - Consumer Price Index, reflects inflation
  • Unemployment - Impacts housing demand

How to Use: Navigate to “Main” to select a REIT and year, and view related economic trends.
